Easy Breathing provides respiratory in-home preventive care and intervention in order to decrease hospital stays and emergency room visits in addtion to ambulance calls. Our services are provided on a 24 hours/7 days a week basis.

A Respiratory Care Practitioner (R.C.P.) is on call after 5:00 pm for any emergency needs.

Services

Respiratory therepy is presented and practiced by only California Licensed Respiratory Care Practitioners in which the following services are provided:

  • Bronchodilater Medication Therapy
  • Control of Respiratory Infections
  • Maintenance of Clear Airways
    1. Aerosol Therapy
    2. Assisted Ventilation
    3. Postural Drainage and Percussion
  • Correction of Ineffective Ventilation
    1. Ventilatory Exercises
  • Improvement in Ambulation
    1. Grader Walking Exercises - with or without Oxygen
    2. Physical Conditional Exercises

Recently, our company has added sleep study services to assist our doctors in the diagnosis of sleep apnea or other sleep abnormalites. This service is provided on a referral basis from your primary care physician.

About Us

Easy Breathing, Inc. was established to provide quality care to patients suffering from COPD(Emphysema), Respiratory Failure, Congestive Heart Failure and other conditions that can affect the overall health of the lungs/heart within the comfort of their homes. Practices

Respiratory therapy is presented and practiced by only qualified California Licensed Respiratory Care Practitioners.

  1. Physician's prescription (Orders).
  2. Brief description of why respiratory therapy services might be medically necessary, including diagnosis and impairments.
  3. Patient provided care, a condition treated in hospital or emergency room.
  4. Patient's bronchial hygiene, expansion therapy and bronchodilator medication therapy for prophylaxis and intervention.

Effective respiratory care (bronchial hygiene, expansion therapy, and/or bronchodilator medication therapy) requires considerable health professional time and effort. The therapy can individualized, but assessment requires frequent peak flows, and spirometry and bronchospasm triggers must be sought from the history, sometimes with each visit.

Acceptance Criteria

  • Patient must have a Physician's order for service.
  • Patient must have a fragile but stable condition.
  • At least four (4) recent hospital/emergency room visits in the past year for difficulty breathing or retained secretions.
  • Patient provided care for a condition treated in hospital or emergency room.
  • Patient's bronchial hygiene, expansion therapy and/or bronchodilator medication therapy for prophylaxis and intervention upon presenting to an emergency room.
